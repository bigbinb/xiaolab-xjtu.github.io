About

Cells need nutrition and energy to growth and divide. Cancer cells have the same needs as normal cells, but use different metabolic programs to proliferate in an uncontrolled manner. In the complex tumor microenvironment (TME) consists of different cullular, including nontumor cells and non-cellular components in and around tumor, diverse regulation of gene transcription, translation, and metabolism occurs in different types of cells. Systematically characterizing the metabolic behavior of cells at the specific genetic and environmental conditions, and understanding how the metabolism is controlled can provide critical insights for therapeutic targeting for cancer therapy. To achieve these goals, we leverage high-throughput sequencing technologies and bioinformatic approaches to investigate many different aspects of cancer metabolism and the tumor microenvironment. Our long-term goal is to design more effective therapeutic strategies that can prevent cancer metastasis while strength antineoplasmic activity of immune cells infiltrating in tumors.

Our group include both computational and experimental platforms. The computational work includes: (1) biomedical data mining (2) studying dynamic genetic and metabolic regulations at different levels. Recently, we are focusing on applying single cell sequencing technologies to study TME and developping information entropy-based models to elucidate the dynamics of regulatory networks driving metabolic reprogramming and immune parameters in the tumor microenviroment. Our experimental work includes: single-cell RNA sequencing, spatial transcriptomics and metabolomics, cell culture, and tumor mouse modelling.
